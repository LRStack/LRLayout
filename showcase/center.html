center

A horizontally-centered content column. Caps its width at --max and centers itself with auto inline margins — the page/content wrapper most layouts sit inside. It has no flexbox of its own, so it composes with anything. (This very page is an .lr-center.)

A capped, centered column

A plain .lr-center caps its child at --max and sits it in the middle of whatever space it's given — here a narrow 24rem column inside a much wider panel.

A readable article column

The everyday use: hold prose to a comfortable measure. Here .lr-center and .lr-stack share one element — the column caps the line length while the stack spaces the content. --pad keeps the text off the edges when the screen is narrower than --max.

Designing with constraints

A column that never grows past a comfortable measure keeps long lines from sprawling across wide screens, where the eye loses its place jumping back to the start of each line.

Because center is just a block, it nests inside any layout and wraps any layout — the same primitive frames a whole page or a single paragraph.
