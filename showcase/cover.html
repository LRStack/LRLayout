cover

A region that fills at least --min of block space (default 100svh) with one vertically-centered principal child and optional header/footer pinned to the top and bottom edges — the classic full-viewport hero, or a dialog parked in the middle of the screen. It's the library's only block-axis centering primitive; centering horizontally is a separate axis you compose (with --items: center or centered text).

A landing hero

The canonical use. The top bar (a split) and the scroll hint pin to the top and bottom edges; the .lr-cover-center block — eyebrow, headline, subtext, buttons — sits in the vertical middle whatever the cover's height. No media queries. Its horizontal centring is composed: that block is a stack with centered text, and its inline bits (chip, buttons) ride lr-cluster lr-justify-center.

A dialog centered in the screen

One child, no marker needed — :only-child centers it vertically. Add --items: center (here via lr-items-center) and it also centers horizontally instead of stretching full-width. The everyday "modal floating in an empty region" pattern, with no positioning.
